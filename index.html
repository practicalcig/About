Our Approach

Theory of Change

Four steps from reaching the most excluded to building a self-sustaining ecosystem of digital empowerment.

01
Reach the Unreached
Identify and recruit the most vulnerable participants through IDP camps, universities, and community networks. Provide device and internet access grants to those who cannot participate without support.
02
Build Real, Applied Skills
Deliver the four-week AI Unlocked curriculum bilingually. Train participants in AI tools, entrepreneurship (BMC, SOSTAC), leadership, and personal development. Every participant leaves with a completed, launch-ready Capstone project.
03
Activate Employment & Self-Employment Pathways
Connect graduates to formal employment. Recruit top performers into 2-month paid internships as junior mentors. During internship they complete ToT and launch their own Capstone — leaving with both a credential and a launched venture.
04
Scale Through a Self-Reinforcing Model
Graduates who become employed contribute to program sustainability. Alumni mentors reduce mentorship costs. Capstone showcases attract new cohorts and institutional partners. The program becomes progressively more self-financing.
